How It Works
♾️Never Run Out (Perpetual)

This is the classic FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early) approach. You accumulate enough savings so that the annual investment returns alone cover your living expenses — forever.

Formula: Target = Annual Expenses ÷ Real Return Rate
Real Return = Investment Return − Inflation

Example: If you spend $50,400/yr and earn 7% with 3% inflation (4% real return), you need $50,400 ÷ 0.04 = $1,260,000.
⏳Spend It All (Drawdown)

You don't need as much saved because you'll spend both returns AND principal over your remaining years. You choose a life expectancy, and the calculator figures out how much you need so your money lasts exactly that long.

Formula: Uses the Present Value of Annuity — it factors in your spending, returns, and years in retirement.

Example: Same expenses, retire at 45, live to 85 (40 years) → you might only need ~$1,060,000 instead of $1.26M.
📊What is "Real Return"?

Your investments might grow at 7%/yr, but inflation eats away ~3%/yr of purchasing power. So your real (inflation-adjusted) return is roughly 4%. This calculator uses real returns so all numbers are in today's dollars — the amounts shown reflect actual purchasing power, not inflated future numbers.
